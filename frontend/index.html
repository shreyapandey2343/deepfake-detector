Understanding Deepfakes

Deepfakes are synthetic media where a person's likeness is replaced using AI. These hyper-realistic videos and audio are often indistinguishable from reality.

Why Created?

Originally developed as AI research to demonstrate realistic face generation.

  • Advancing computer vision
  • Improving film VFX
  • Creating digital avatars
  • Enhancing VR experiences

Positive Uses

  • Film and entertainment VFX
  • Historical figure recreation
  • Voice restoration for patients
  • AI avatars in gaming

Modern Misuse

Increasingly used for harmful ethical and security concerns.

  • Political misinformation
  • Celebrity impersonation scams
  • Non-consensual media
  • Identity fraud

Did You Know?

What does "Deep" refer to?
Deep Learning: Neural networks with many layers.
What is a GAN?
Generative Adversarial Network: Two networks competing.
How to spot a deepfake?
Look for unnatural blinking or poor lip-syncing.
Is it illegal?
Tech is legal, but fraud/defamation is illegal.
